Data on-demand: The key to unlock value for the telco of the future

Telefonica UK has been delivering revenue and customer growth since 2017

Telefonica UK drives consistent revenue and customer growth, is projected to eclipse £6b in revenue in 2019 and is one of 4 key markets to focus in the new company’s 5-point plan. These results are driven by its O2 brand recognition, strong customer service, and innovative commercial strategies such as Custom Plans in Contract.

Moreover, with the advent of 5G technology, O2 is positioned to expand its product portfolio to create and diversify new revenue stream from customers and businesses, so significant funding is necessary to sustain 5G network deployment and realise its future plan.

However, while Contracts drive growth, PAYG top-ups are limiting funding

Top-ups are time-consuming and don’t meet the needs of today’s customer, who seeks quick, easy and convenient services. Most top-up user journeys involve at maximum 4 steps, becoming a real barrier to sell to customers, who are relying more on data and that's set to continue.

But, although in decline and low in ARPU, PAYG can still unlock growth for the business, because is a source of Contracts and offers the flexibility that customers want. So, there’s still an opportunity to redefine PAYG product to work in our and customers favour.

So how can Telefonica UK drive revenue to fund future investments and solidify customer loyalty?

Telefonica UK can simplify its Pay As You Go one step further: scrap top ups altogether and integrate an SMS chatbot with payment capabilities to provide an easier and quicker way for customers to buy data. This is data on-demand, an initiative that not only can now capture revenue on the spot, but also can develop direct relationships with customers, meeting customers expectations and building a competitive moat to fund and realise Telefonica’s digital transformation. For the purpose of this report, this initiative is named Pay As You Go Prime.
